# Sideways: Reduce

Simplify, subtract, remove, clarify, focus on essentials

**When to use:** Use when overwhelmed by complexity, dealing with too many options, or when clarity is needed. Best for cutting through noise and finding the core.

## Instructions

1. Pick ONE strategy at RANDOM from the list below - do NOT choose the "best fit"
2. Present the strategy card: 🎴 Strategy: "[strategy text]"
3. Generate 5 highly personalized follow-up questions based on the user's specific situation
   - Reference concrete details from their context (technologies, constraints, concerns)
   - Make each question actionable and directly relevant to what they're working on

## Strategies

- "What's the boring solution that works?"
- "What if less is the solution?"
- "2 hours, not 2 weeks. What's different?"
- "What can you not do?"
- "Delete half. Which half?"
- "What if you stopped?"
- "What's the one thing?"
- "Which problem are you avoiding by solving this one?"
- "What's the smallest version that matters?"
- "Remove the middle"
- "Skip this"
- "Subtract to reveal"
- "What's the smallest change?"
- "What does deletion reveal?"
- "Read it aloud. Still make sense?"
- "First principle or first habit?"
- "Core or decoration?"
- "Essential or familiar?"
- "What's the tax?"
- "What constraint are you pretending doesn't exist?"
- "What decision are you avoiding?"
